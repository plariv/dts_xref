Most tests only tell us what a test-taker knows. Lectical assessments are a major advance over other forms of assessment, because they fulfill three important objectives. They tell us: </p>
 341                  <ol>
 342                    <li>what the test-taker <span class="emphasis">knows</span>; </li>
 343                    <li>how the test-taker <em class="emphasis">applies</em>  what s/he knows; and</li>

To this end, we have collected and analyzed literally thousands of clinical interviews and essays, gradually building the knowledge base required to understand how specific skills develop over time. This research is widely published in peer reviewed journals, books, and on the web. To learn more about it,  go to the <a href="articles.html">articles</a><a href="../PDF/LRJA_11-4gen.pdf"></a> page.                </p>

 383                <div class="AccordionPanelContent">
 384                  <p>Our Certified Lectical Analysts undergo extensive training. To retain their certification they must maintain an agreement rate with a Master Lectical  Analyst of 85% within 1/4 of a lectical level. This is a  high agreement rate—the gold standard in developmental assessment. This level of inter-rater agreement means that individuals who take our assessments can be confident that scores awarded to their performances are reliable within the range of 1/4 of a lectical level.</p>
 385                  <p>Keep in mind, however, that <span class="emphasis">we can only score performances</span>. A number of factors can contribute to variations in performance. For example, if an individual tries  to complete an assessment with a toddler on her lap, after a poor night's sleep, or while sipping a glass of wine, her performance is likely to be compromised. Similarly, if she does not carefully follow the instructions for an assessment, she may not provide  adequate responses to questions. This almost always results in a score that is lower than it could be.</p>
