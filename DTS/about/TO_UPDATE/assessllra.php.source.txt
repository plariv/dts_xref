  <h1>ABOUT THE LECTICAL LEADERSHIP REASONING ASSESSMENT*  </h1>
  <p class="pspaced">The LLRA is an assessment of reasoning about leadership. It examines how individuals think about a range of leadership qualities and how these relate to their overall conception of a good leader.</p>
  <h3 class="pspaced">General information</h3>
  <p class="pspaced"><em>For adolescents (12+) and adults</em></p>
  <p class="pspaced">The LLRA asks  test-takers to write short essays about four self-chosen leadership qualities (selected from a set of pull-down menus). It then asks them to discuss how these qualities work together to define great leadership. </p>
  <p class="pspaced">The LLRA can be custiomized by specifying the qualities test-takers will write about. For example, if you are ecaluating a particular curriculum, you can ask clients to write about the specific categories/qualities targeted by that curriculum.</p>
  <h3 class="pspaced">Check it out</h3>
<p class="pspaced">You can examine the LLRA by logging on to the DTS site and choosing <a href="../landing.php">select an assessment</a>, under the &quot;take an assessment&quot; item in the main menu on the left. You will need to enter information into the starred fields before you will be allowed to move from one page of the assessment to the next. Feel free to cut and paste blocks of random text into the essay fields.</p>
  <h3 class="pspaced">Reliability and validity</h3>
  <p class="pspaced">The LLRA, like all Lectical Assessments, reliably distinguishes 8â€“10 adult developmental phases, where each phase represents 1/4 of a developmental level. All Lectical Assessments have very similar levels of reliability, because they are scored with the same developmental assessment system, the <a href="../PDF/LAS.pdf">LAS</a>.</p>
  <p class="pspaced">Lectical assessments address two broad forms of validity: <strong><em>construct</em></strong> validity and <strong><em>ecological</em></strong> validity. First, like all Lectical Assessments, the LLRA is scored with the LAS, which is (1) based on a strong theory of development called <a href="http://www.gse.harvard.edu/~ddl/publication.htm">Dynamic Skill Theory</a>, and (2) has been submitted to a number of rigorous tests of its ability to capture the developmental construct described in that theory. These tests have shown that the LAS does a very good job capturing this dimension. To view the evidence, see the refereed papers under the articles tab on the <a href="../_about/articles.php">articles</a> page and articles by our colleagues on the leadership <a href="referenceslead.html">references</a> page.</p>
  <p class="pspaced">Second, the ecological validity of the LLRA is apparent in the relevance of (1)  its contentâ€”its questions are about leadership; (2) the skills required to complete itâ€”skills for thinking about leadership, perspective-taking, clear thinking, communicating, and critical reflection; and (3) the scores and feedback provided in its reports.</p>
  <p class="pspaced">To learn more about <a href="http://theodawson.net/?tag=statistical-reliability">reliability</a> and <a href="http://theodawson.net/?tag=validity">validity</a>, see Dr. Dawson's <a href="http://theodawson.net/">blog</a>.</p>
  <h3 class="pspaced">Reports</h3>
  <p class="pspaced">Lectical assessments are distinguished by the accuracy of LAS scores and the quality of our feedback. Before we launch a Lectical Assessment, we carefully review the relevant research literature, then study how the skills and concepts targeted by the assessment develop over time, using a research methodology called <a href="../_about/devmaieutics.php">developmental maieutics</a>. The approach allows us to describe what  targeted skills and concepts &quot;look like&quot; in each developmental phase, and to craft feedback and learning suggestions that are specific to each phase of performance. </p>
  <p class="pspaced">In addition to general feedback related to the phase of a given performance, each LLRA report includes personal feedback, including comments on strengths and areas for growth and suggestions for learning and development.</p>
  <p class="pspaced">Reports are generally delivered within 5â€“7 business days. We send an email notification each time an assessment is finalized. </p>
