          <h1>PROFILE OF THEO DAWSON</h1>
      <p><strong>I am a cognitive scientist, psychometrician, web developer, and recognized leader in the assessment of management and leadership skills.</strong> In 1993, concerned about how little educators knew about adult learning, I left the business world to study how adults learn essential cognitive skills. My award-winning dissertation introduced a novel method for measuring and describing how such skills develop over the course of the life-span. For the last decade I have conducted extensive validation studies of this method, while using it to study and document how real managers' skills—<em>such as skills required for leadership, decision-making, ethical reasoning, and critical thinking</em>—develop over time. I have also used this method to analyze the task demands of managers' jobs, making it possible to ensure a better match between job requirements and managers’ skills. Now, I would like to bring my skills to new organizations, (1) working closely with consulting firms and in-house trainers to ensure an optimal match between managers’ skills and job demands; (2) improving college admissions and outcomes with high quality assessments of students' reasoning; and (3)  working with researchers to increase our understanding of how people learn important life and workplace skills.
