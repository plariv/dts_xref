 122  <h1 class="indent10">Loving kindness meditation, cont.</h1>
 123    <p>Take a very comfortable posture. One of the aims in this meditation is to feel good, so make your posture relaxed and comfortable. Begin to focus around the solar plexus, your chest area, your &quot;heart center&quot;. Breathe in and out from that area, as if you are breathing from the heart center and as if all experience is happening from there. Anchor your mindfulness only on the sensations at your heart center. </p>
 124      <p>Breathing in and out from the heart center, begin by generating this kind feeling toward yourself. Feel any areas of mental blockage or numbness, self-judgment, self-hatred. Then drop beneath that to the place where we care for ourselves, where we want strength and health and safety for ourselves. Continuing to breathe in and out, use either these traditional phrases or ones you choose yourself. Say or think them several times.</p>
 125      <blockquote>
 126        <p>May I be free from inner and outer harm and danger. May I be safe and protected.<br />
 127          May I be free of mental suffering or distress. <br />
 128          May I be happy.<br />
 129          May I be free of physical pain and suffering. <br />
 130          May I be healthy and strong.<br />
 131          May I be able to live in this world happily, peacefully, joyfully, with ease.</p>
 132      </blockquote>
 133      <p>Next, move to a person who most invites the feeling of pure unconditional loving kindness, the love that does not depend on getting anything back. This person is usually someone we consider a mentor, a benefactor, an elder. It might be a parent, grandparent, teacher, someone toward whom it takes no effort to feel respect and reverence, someone who immediately elicits the feeling of care. Repeat the phrases for this person: &quot;May she be safe and protected…&quot;</p>
 134      <p>After feeling strong unconditional love for the benefactor, move to a person you regard as a dear friend and repeat the phrases again, breathing in and out of your heart center. </p>
 135      <p>Now move to a neutral person, someone for whom you feel neither strong like nor dislike. As you repeat the phrases, allow yourself to feel tenderness, loving care for their welfare.</p>
 136      <p>Now move to someone you have difficulty with—hostile feelings, resentments. Repeat the phrases for this person. If you have difficulty doing this, you can say before the phrases, &quot;To the best of my ability I wish that you be…&quot; If you begin to feel ill will toward this person, return to the benefactor and let the loving kindness arise again. Then return to this person. </p>
 137      <p>Let the phrases spread through your whole body, mind, and heart. </p>
 138      <p>After the difficult person, radiate loving kindness out to all beings. Stay in touch with the ember of warm, tender loving-kindness at the center of your being, and begin to visualize or engender a felt sense of all living beings. The traditional phrases are these:</p>
 139      <blockquote>
 140        <p>May all beings be safe, happy, healthy, live joyously.<br />
 141          May all living beings be safe, happy, healthy, live joyously.<br />
 142          May all breathing beings be safe, happy, healthy, live joyously.<br />
 143          May all individuals be safe, happy, healthy, live joyously.<br />
 144          May all beings in existence be safe, happy, healthy, live joyously.<br />
 145        </p>
 146    </blockquote>
 147      <p>Here is an alternative way to practice this meditation: <br />
 148      </p>
 149      <p>Begin with yourself. Calm the mind/heart and find the center of your being. Generate warm, gentle, loving feelings for yourself:<br />
 150      </p>
 151      <blockquote>
 152        <p>May I be safe from harm.<br />
 153          May I be happy just as I am.<br />
 154          May I be peaceful with whatever is happening.<br />
 155          May I be healthy and strong.<br />
 156          May I care for myself in this ever-changing world graciously, joyously.<br />
 157        </p>
 158      </blockquote>
 159      <p>From yourself, move out spaciously into your immediate surroundings. Include every living being within this circle:<br />
 160      </p>
 161      <p>May all beings in the air, on land, and in the water be safe, happy, healthy, and free from suffering.</p>
 162      <p>*Modified from Steven Smith, <a href="http://www.contemplativemind.org/practices/subnav/kindness.htm">Contemplative Mind</a></p>
